Who is Satoshi Nakamoto?

Satoshi Nakamoto, the pseudonym behind the creation of Bitcoin, remains one of the most mysterious and enigmatic figures in the world of technology and finance.

Here's a timeline of Satoshi's appearances:

  • 2008 - Satoshi Nakamoto publishes the Bitcoin white paper and releases the first version of the Bitcoin software.
  • January 2009 - Nakamoto mines the first block of the Bitcoin blockchain, also known as the "genesis block."
  • January 2009 - Nakamoto posts on a cryptography mailing list, announcing the release of the Bitcoin software and inviting others to join the network.
  • February 2009 - Nakamoto posts on the same mailing list, discussing the design of the Bitcoin system.
  • November 2009 - Nakamoto posts on the Bitcoin Forum, announcing a "minor bug" in the software and providing an update.
  • December 2010 - Nakamoto disappears from the public eye, handing over control of the Bitcoin code to other developers.
  • December 2010 - Nakamoto posts on the Bitcoin Forum, stating that he has "moved on to other things" and that he will no longer be involved in the development of Bitcoin.
  • April 2011 - Nakamoto's last known communication, in which they respond to a query on the Bitcoin Forum about the possibility of a 51% attack on the Bitcoin network.
  • 2011 - The first bitcoin transaction is made between Satoshi and Hal Finney, a developer and early adopter of the technology.
  • 2014 - An Australian entrepreneur, Craig Wright, claims to be Satoshi Nakamoto, but his claim is met with skepticism and is not confirmed.
  • 2016 - A book by investigative journalist Andrew O'Hagan, The Satoshi Affair, is published, which details his attempts to uncover the true identity of Satoshi Nakamoto.
  • 2020 - A Japanese man, named by the media as Satoshi Nakamoto, denies that he is the man behind the pseudonym.
  • To date - Satoshi Nakamoto's identity has never been confirmed and it is still unknown who or who are the individuals behind this pseudonym. Additionally, the timeline provided is not exhaustive, there might be some other events that are not included.
